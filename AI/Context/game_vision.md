# Game Vision – Braziliation

## One-line pitch

**Braziliation** is a 2D pixel-art platform/action game set in a Brazilian dieselpunk post-apocalyptic world, with SNES-era visual style and tight, responsive gameplay.

## Core pillars

1. **Brazilian identity** – Setting, lore, and tone are rooted in Brazil: landscapes, culture, language, and post-collapse factions. Not generic post-apocalypse.
2. **Dieselpunk aesthetic** – Industrial, mechanical, steam-and-oil tech; rugged machinery, improvised weapons, and heavy machinery in a fallen world.
3. **Pixel art, SNES-inspired** – Restricted palette, clear silhouettes, readable animation. Every pixel counts.
4. **Platform/action** – Movement and combat feel good first; level design and systems support mastery and exploration.
5. **Solo dev + AI** – Architecture and documentation must support one developer and AI agents working together without confusion.

## Target experience

- **Feel:** Responsive controls, satisfying combat, clear feedback. Player always knows why they died or succeeded.
- **Mood:** Gritty, hopeful, occasionally dark; Brazilian resilience and humor can show through.
- **Scope:** Manageable for a solo developer; modular systems so content (levels, enemies, items) can grow without rewriting core.

## Out of scope (for now)

- Online multiplayer.
- Open-world; level-based or hub-based structure is preferred.
- Realistic 3D or non–pixel-art art style.
